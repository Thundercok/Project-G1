# Weapons

## Crowbar

![Crowbar](images/crowbar.png)

The starter weapon. Model generated by `Tools/blender/build_crowbar.py`: hex-stock
shaft in worn red, bare-steel curved hook and chisel tip, origin at the grip.

### Viewmodel setup (built by `G1SceneBuilder`)

```
Player
└─ ViewCamera (fov 75, near 0.05)
   └─ WeaponHolder          ← Crowbar.cs lives here, local pos (0.32, −0.34, 0.55)
      └─ Crowbar.fbx        ← local rot (−62, 18, 12), colliders stripped
```

`Crowbar.cs` animates **the holder**, never the model, so swapping the model for a
better one later changes nothing about the feel.

### The swing

Code-driven (no animation clips), one coroutine, three phases:

| Phase | Window | Motion |
|---|---|---|
| Windup | 0 → 0.07 s | ease to a small back-left cock |
| Strike | 0.07 → 0.16 s | quadratic ease-in to the hit pose (fast at contact) |
| Impact | at 0.16 s | raycast from the camera, range 1.9 m |
| Recover | 0.16 → 0.42 s | slerp back to rest |

Holding fire re-swings at the ~0.42 s cadence — close to HL1's crowbar rhythm.
The impact applies `damage` (25) to any `Breakable` in the parent chain of the hit
collider and an impulse to rigidbodies.

There's also a movement-driven viewmodel **bob**: amplitude and frequency scale
with grounded horizontal speed, zeroed in the air. All numbers are public fields —
tune in the Inspector during Play mode, then copy values back.

### Breakables

`Breakable.cs`: crates take hits (shrinking slightly as damage feedback), then
shatter into 8 physics-simulated shards that reuse the crate's material and
despawn after 5 s. HL crate ritual: fully supported.

## 9mm pistol

![Pistol](images/pistol.png)

Generated by `Tools/blender/create_pistol.py`: slide, trigger, and magazine are
**separate rigid parts** skinned to a 4-bone armature (`root`, `slide`,
`trigger`, `magazine`), so the weapon animates mechanically like the real thing.
The grip sits at the origin and the barrel faces −Y in Blender, which imports
into Unity pointing +Z — the viewmodel mounts with **identity rotation**, no
offset fixups.

Animation takes baked into the FBX:

| Clip | Length | Content |
|---|---|---|
| `Idle` | 2 s loop | barely-perceptible hold sway |
| `Fire` | 0.33 s | trigger pull + full slide cycle + muzzle kick |
| `Reload` | 1.6 s | mag drops along the grip rake, reseats, slide racks |

`G1Pistol.cs` (on `PistolHolder`, extends `WeaponBase`): semi-auto (0.2 s
interval), 17-round clip + 68 reserve, `R` or empty-fire to reload, hitscan via
the shared `RayHit`/`ApplyHit` helpers (8 dmg — HL1 9mm — plus a physics
impulse). Animations are driven by `Animator.CrossFade` on the model's Animator
(`Idle` default; `Fire`/`Reload` states exit back to `Idle` automatically —
controller generated by `G1SceneBuilder.MakePistolController`). Ammo shows via
a debug `OnGUI` label until a real HUD exists.

## WeaponBase & switching

`WeaponBase` owns what every held weapon shares: camera/movement wiring, the
cursor-lock input gate, viewmodel bob, and the raycast + damage/impulse
helpers (`RayHit`, `ApplyHit` → `IDamageable`, see
[combat-and-health.md](combat-and-health.md)). A weapon implements one method:
`HandleInput()`.

`WeaponSwitcher` (on the camera) activates exactly one holder: keys **1/2/3** or
the scroll wheel.

## 9mm SMG (Submachine Gun)

![SMG](images/smg.png)

Generated by `Tools/blender/create_smg.py`: stock, receiver, grip, handguard, barrel, sights, and trigger guard make up the static mesh. The bolt assembly (charging handle) and magazine are separate raked parts rigged to a 4-bone armature (`root`, `bolt`, `trigger`, `magazine`).

Animation takes baked into the FBX:

| Clip | Length | Content |
|---|---|---|
| `Idle` | 2 s loop | slight hold breathing sway |
| `Fire` | 0.13 s loop | trigger pull + rapid bolt recoil cycle + muzzle kick |
| `Reload` | 2 s | magazine drops, new magazine seats, charging handle is pulled and released |

`G1Smg.cs` (on `SmgHolder`, extends `WeaponBase`): automatic fire (0.1 s fire interval), 50-round magazine + 150 reserve, 5 damage per round. Wired to key **3**.

## Adding the next weapon (Shotgun is next on the roadmap)

1. Model it in a `create_shotgun.py` following the weapon script's pattern — origin at the grip, barrel toward −Y, parts split per animated bone.
2. Write a `G1Shotgun : WeaponBase` — napping loader reload, multi-pellet spread.
3. Register it in `G1SceneBuilder.BuildPlayer` (holder + controller) and append it to the `WeaponSwitcher` array.
